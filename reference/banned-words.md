# Banned Words and Phrases

Use this as the first pass on every consumer-facing asset. If a phrase appears in the left column, presume it is banned unless the right column conditions are met. The "why" is craft (vagueness, unsubstantiated, dark pattern, brand-risk) — not legal review. Get your own compliance and partner bank review before shipping anything.

| Banned phrase | Why / safer alternative |
|---|---|
| "Free" (standalone) | Implies no cost ever and almost always falls apart on contact with a fee schedule. Safer: "$0 monthly fee" + footnote listing every condition that does have a fee (out-of-network ATM, foreign transaction, expedited delivery, paper statements, overdraft). |
| "No fees" | Almost never literally true. Safer: "No monthly maintenance fee" + specific list of fees that do exist. |
| "0% fees forever" / "fees never" | "Forever" claims age badly. Future fee changes are predictable. Safer: "$0 monthly fee" with a "fees subject to change with notice" clause. |
| "Guaranteed" returns / yield / earnings | Deposit yields are variable. Guaranteeing them is a brand-risk line. Safer: "[X]% APY as of [date]. Variable rate." |
| "Risk-free" | Almost never usable. Even insured deposits have edge cases. Safer: state the actual coverage. |
| "FDIC insured" (without partner bank) | The fintech is not the insured entity. Safer: "Banking services provided by [Partner Bank Name], Member FDIC." |
| "We are FDIC insured" / "[Brand] is FDIC insured" | Same as above. The fintech is not the bank. Safer: see above. |
| "Your money is FDIC protected" (used by non-bank) | Implies fintech-level coverage. Safer: "Your deposits are held at [Bank], Member FDIC, where they are eligible for FDIC insurance up to $250,000 per depositor, per insured bank, per ownership category." |
| "Instant" transfer / "instant" payout | Only usable if technically true 100% of the time. The moment one transfer is delayed at scale, the claim becomes a pattern of failed promises. Safer: "Most transfers complete in under [X] minutes" + state the exception window. |
| "Best APY" / "highest APY" | Superlatives need an effective date and methodology. Safer: "[X]% APY as of [date], among the top rates in [category] per [source, date]." |
| "Highest yield" | Same as above. |
| "High yield" (no APY shown) | If you are talking about a savings rate, name the rate. Safer: "[X]% APY" stated explicitly. |
| "Earn up to X%" (no conditions) | Top-tier conditions matter. Safer: "Earn up to [X]% APY. Top tier requires balance of $[Y] and [other condition]." |
| "Save up to $X" (no methodology) | A claim without math is a claim without credibility. Safer: show the math. "Customers saved an average of $[X] in fees in [period] vs [comparable account at named competitor]. Methodology: [link]." |
| "Get rich" / "build wealth fast" | Wealth claims on deposit products are off-pattern and read as bait. Safer: stick to the actual feature. |
| "Pays you to bank" | Implies guaranteed earnings. Safer: state the rate or rewards structure with conditions. |
| "Bank-grade security" | Vague and often inaccurate when the fintech relies on partner-bank security plus its own stack. Safer: name specific certifications (SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001) and link to the security page. |
| "Military-grade encryption" | Marketing fluff with no defined standard. Safer: "256-bit AES encryption in transit and at rest." |
| "100% safe" / "completely safe" | Absolute safety claims fail every screenshot test. Safer: describe specific protections (FDIC pass-through up to $250K, fraud monitoring, customer reimbursement policy with terms). |
| "Money you can trust" | Vague trust claim with nothing behind it. Safer: name the partner bank, name the certifications, link the disclosures. |
| "Beats your bank" | Comparison without source and date. Safer: "[X]% APY vs the [Y]% national average per FDIC weekly rate as of [date]." |
| "Trusted by millions" (no source) | Substantiation required. Safer: "[N] customers as of [date]" with the count tied to active users, not lifetime signups. |
| "Award-winning" (no specifics) | Vague unless cited. Safer: "[Award], [year], by [organization]." |
| "Built for you" / "designed for you" | Fluff, not banned, flag for editing. |
| "Switch in seconds" / "open in seconds" | Often inaccurate. KYC, funding, and partner-bank checks add minutes or hours. Safer: "Most accounts are opened in under [X] minutes" + condition. |
| "Approved instantly" | Same. KYC sometimes requires manual review. Safer: "Most applicants are approved in under [X] minutes." |
| "No credit check" (when there is any) | If a soft inquiry occurs, the claim is misleading. Safer: "No hard credit check. We may run a soft inquiry that does not affect your credit score." |
| "Guaranteed approval" | Banned. Approval is conditional on KYC, funding source, and underwriting. Safer: omit. |
| "Pre-approved" | Heavily restricted. Use with care and an actual eligibility basis. Safer: "Eligible for [product] based on [criteria]." |
| "Lowest fees" / "cheapest" | Comparison superlative requires source and date. Safer: cite the comparison with date. |
| "100% transparent" | Fluff plus risk: implies disclosure of everything, then any non-disclosure becomes a credibility hit. Safer: link the fee schedule and disclosures. |
| "Always-on support" / "24/7 support" | If support has any limit, this is misleading. Safer: state actual hours and channels. |
| "Hidden fees we don't have" | Comparative fee claims need substantiation. Safer: state your fees, link the schedule, do not characterize competitors without dated sources. |
| "Better than a bank" | Often false; the fintech is not a bank. Safer: state specific differences (e.g. higher APY at specific rate, lower specific fee). |
| "Your money grows" | Implies guaranteed return. Safer: "Earn [X]% APY as of [date], variable." |
| "Cash back guaranteed" | If conditioned on category or merchant, "guaranteed" is misleading. Safer: "[X]% cash back on [eligible categories] up to $[Y] per [period]. Conditions apply." |
| "Up to $X cash back" | Requires the conditions to reach the cap. Safer: state the cap, the conditions, and the exclusions. |
| "Build credit guaranteed" | Banned. Credit-building depends on bureau reporting and customer behavior. Safer: "Reports payment history to [bureaus]. Results vary." |
| "Investment" / "investing" applied to a deposit account | Deposits are not investments. Safer: "savings account" or "deposit account." |
| "Yield" alone (instead of APY) | If you are talking about a savings rate, name it as APY. Safer: "[X]% APY." |
| "Get paid early" without conditions | Common neobank claim. Conditions: depends on employer's payroll file submission. Safer: "Get paid up to 2 days early when your employer submits payroll early. Timing is not guaranteed." |
| "Overdraft-free" | If overdraft is just renamed (cash advance, paycheck advance), the claim breaks down on inspection. Safer: name the product correctly and disclose fees. |
| "Free money" / "easy money" | Banned across the board. |
| "Limited time" / "ends soon" without truth | False scarcity is a dark pattern. Safer: only used when there is a real, dated promo end. |
| "Only N spots left" without truth | Same. Safer: only with real inventory. |

## How to use this list

1. Run every consumer-facing draft past this list before sending it for any internal review.
2. If a banned phrase appears, replace with the safer alternative.
3. Add anything new your partner bank's marketing team flags. The list is a living craft document, not a legal artifact. Get your own compliance and partner bank review before publishing anything.
